Background

Context & Motivation

The lack of knowledge and awareness of poor indoor air quality presents significant challenges in identifying/mitigating potential health risks. Many environments may contain harmful pollutants, allergens, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that go unnoticed, which can lead to long-term exposure and adverse health effects.

According to a 2021 study published by the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), particulate matter ranks as the first health risk factor among 88 considered.
DALYs for a disease are the sum of years of life lost due to premature mortality YLLs, and years lived with a disability YLDs.

Deliverable 1

Project Overview

Problem Statement

Design and prototype a low-cost indoor air quality sensing solution that can accurately provide live data and long-term air quality trends to the public through website & mobile application formats.

Objectives

• Functionality: Must meet RESET Air quality monitor standard
 o Measure parameters: PM2.5, (TVOC, CO2, Temperature, Humidity)
• Analyze data to show data trends over desired time ranges for each user, easy to access/interpret.
• Easy to install sensor modules

Constraints

• Project Budget: $190/person = $760
• Time: Detailed design by end of term of all subsystems
• Weight: 0.4kg [subject to change based on solution implementation]
• Accuracy of sensing:
  o PM2.5: +/- 5% ug
  o TVOC: +/- 15% ppb
  o CO2: +/- 5% ppm
  o Temperature: +/- 1 degree
  o Humidity: +/- 5% RH
• Frequency of readings: 1 reading/minute

Criteria

• Performance: sensor measurement accuracy
• Efficiency: battery life duration
• Show data trends over desired time ranges
• Coverage of public indoor spaces
• Size: Minimum size/form factor possible, "blend in to environment"
