Deliverable 1

Finalize the Problem

Problem Statement

• Lack of knowledge and awareness of poor indoor air quality leading to potential health risks.
• Inability to enforce indoor air quality standards (such as RESET certification) due to lack of infrastructure related to widespread sensing.

Examples of Problem (from our experience)

• Ex: crowded gym (high population density) – high levels of CO2
• Ex: old building – mould or other hazardous particles
• Ex: kitchens (furnaces) – CO
• Ex: hospital – want good air

Additional Notes

• Identify source of contaminants within indoor spaces or problematic areas in a large indoor space
• Data is power – we can develop trends and open source the data to the public
• Getting better or worse over time?
• Individual exposure to harmful contaminants

Deliverable 2

Need, Problem Definition, C&C

The Need

“Each year, 3.2 million people die prematurely from illnesses attributable to household air pollution. The combined effects of ambient air pollution and household air pollution are associated with 6.7 million premature deaths annually.”

Problem Statement

The lack of knowledge and awareness of poor indoor air quality presents significant challenges in identifying/mitigating potential health risks. Many environments may contain harmful pollutants, allergens, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that go unnoticed, which can lead to long-term exposure and adverse health effects.

Constraints

• Budget: $190/person = $760
• Time: Detailed design by end of term of all subsystems
• Functionality: Must meet RESET Air quality monitor standard
  o Measure parameters: PM2.5, TVOC, CO2, Temperature, Humidity
• Regulatory: follow relevant ISO standards for our product
• (design intent) Cost per Sensing Unit: $20

Criteria

• Performance: accuracy of measurement
• Efficiency: (if a battery is used) Battery life: 1 year
  o Otherwise: tethered
• Show data trends over desired time ranges
• Wide coverage of public indoor spaces “zones”
• User experience:
  o Easy to swap batteries
  o Any sensors that last less than 5 years should be swappable
  o Easy to access/interpret data
  o Easy to set up/install, minimal equipment required, product clearly shows when connected/disconnected, on/off
• Durability: Product life of 5 years (permanent component wear/tear)
