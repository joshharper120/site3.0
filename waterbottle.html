Smart Water Bottle

More about Project
About

Project Summary

The smart water bottle is capable of measuring the volume of water consumed over time, and track it through an iOS app. This project was completed in under 36 hours for Hack the North 2021.

Ideation

Background

The goal was to provide users with an accurate representation of their water consumption, in hopes it would encourage them to drink more. As university students, we noticed we often get consumed in our work and forget to drink water. We believe that tracking it would be the best way to improve it.

Development

Product Design

The device uses an ESP32 connected to an ultrasonic sensor. This was concealed in a bottle cap that I designed and 3D-printed on my Ender 3 V2. The threads for the cap were reverse-engineered from a bottle I had on hand, and turned out quite nicely.

Components

External Power Supply
ESP32
Ultrasonic Sensor
Dupont Wires

Wireless Connectivity

I connected the ESP32 to my local internet in C++, and transmitted the data to Thingspeak by MATLAB for processing. My team mate then linked the Thingspeak API to an iOS application he created (UI shown above).
