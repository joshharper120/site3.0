About

Project Summary

The smart water bottle is capable of measuring the volume of water consumed over time, and track it through an iOS app. This project was completed in under 36 hours for Hack the North 2021.

The goal was to provide users with an accurate representation of their water consumption, in hopes it would encourage them to drink more. As university students, we noticed we often get consumed in our work and forget to drink water. We believe that tracking it would be the best way to improve it.

Development

Product Design

The first prototype was created to test the electronics. At this stage, motor performance testing was performed and the connections were verified.

Theory

The device works by approximating the volume of air in the water bottle as a function of time. As this air amount increases, the removed water is measured and quantified as a volume of water consumed. Using the principle of ultra sonic sensing, this data can be simply calculated by placing an ultrasonic sensor in the water bottle lid.

Wireless Connectivity

I connected the ESP32 to my local internet in C++, and transmitted the data to Thingspeak by MATLAB for processing. My team mate then linked the Thingspeak API to an iOS application he created (UI shown above).

Results

Key Takeaways

After a long night of work, everything worked! The device successfully received and transmitted data to and from the iOS app my teammate created.
Some verification test cases demonstrated that it was capable of accurately measuring fluid volumes once settled. Therefore, to ensure accuracy we were required to implement adjustment factors and data rejection algorithms to account for incorrect data readings.
